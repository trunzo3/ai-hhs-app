# Peer Review: The Draft-Verify-Revise Habit

AI produces plausible-sounding content that can be wrong, incomplete, outdated, or just not quite right for your specific context. The Peer Review habit is the IQmeetEQ approach to using AI outputs responsibly: Draft → Verify → Revise.

## Why Peer Review Matters

AI doesn't know what it doesn't know. It will produce confident-sounding text even when it's working from incomplete information. For HHS work — where accuracy affects client outcomes, legal compliance, and public trust — treating AI output as a first draft to be verified, not a final product to be delivered, is essential.

The Peer Review habit also makes you a better AI user over time. When you routinely check AI outputs, you notice patterns in where AI tends to be weak (numbers, local context, recent changes, nuanced judgment calls) and where it tends to be strong (structure, clear writing, generating options, explaining concepts).

## Draft

Use AI to produce a strong first draft. Don't aim for perfection in the prompt — aim for something useful enough to react to. The goal is to get words on the page that you can improve, not to get AI to write your document for you.

## Verify

Before you send or use the output, check it. Specifically:

**Factual accuracy:** Are dates, regulations, statistics, and references correct? AI can hallucinate facts confidently. Any specific claim should be checked against the source.

**Local accuracy:** Does this match your county's specific policies, processes, and context? AI doesn't know your county. It knows general patterns.

**Judgment calls:** Did the AI make judgment calls that you'd make differently? AI tends toward the middle of the road. Your specific context might require a different approach.

**Tone and voice:** Does this sound like you and like your organization? AI's default voice is corporate and generic. You may need to adjust.

## Revise

Edit the output to make it accurate, appropriate, and yours. Most AI drafts need at least light editing. The best AI users treat AI output as scaffolding — useful structure that they then fill in and refine.

## The Critique Scaffold Prompt

One useful Peer Review technique is to paste your AI-generated draft into a different AI session and ask it to critique the output. A useful critique prompt:

"Here is a draft I'm working on. Please identify: (1) anything that might be factually incorrect, (2) any gaps or missing information, (3) anything that seems unclear or could be misunderstood, and (4) the weakest argument or claim in the document."

This cross-checking approach catches errors and blind spots that are hard to see when you're close to the content.
