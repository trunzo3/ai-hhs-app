# APS and Adult Protective Services Prompts for HHS Managers

## Important Data Safety Note

Adult Protective Services involves vulnerable clients with sensitive health, financial, and personal information. APS cases involve allegations of abuse, neglect, or exploitation — all of which are highly confidential. Before using AI for any APS-related task, run a Red/Yellow/Green assessment. Client names, case numbers, diagnoses, financial information, and specific case details are Red — never include them in AI prompts. Use generalized descriptions and hypotheticals for any task involving specific client situations.

## Policy and Regulatory Tasks (Green)

**Welfare and Institutions Code interpretation:**
"Explain [specific WIC code section] in plain language for APS supervisors. What does this require us to do? What discretion do we have? Are there common misunderstandings?"

**Understanding adult abuse categories:**
"Describe the different types of elder and dependent adult abuse under California law in plain language. What distinguishes physical abuse from neglect? What is self-neglect and how is it different?"

**Cross-reporting requirements:**
"Explain cross-reporting requirements for APS in California. When must we report to law enforcement? When to Licensing? What are the timelines?"

## Supervisory and Management Tasks (Green-Yellow)

**Case consultation structure (without identifying details):**
"Help me create a framework for APS supervisory case consultations. What questions should supervisors ask to assess risk? What factors indicate a case needs escalation?"

**Safety planning concepts:**
"What are evidence-based approaches to safety planning with self-neglecting adults who refuse services? What does the research say about effective engagement strategies?"

**Difficult conversations with clients:**
"Help me prepare for a conversation with an adult client who is refusing services but appears to be at significant risk [describe scenario type without identifying details]. What approaches might be effective? What should I avoid?"

## Leadership and Communication Tasks (Green)

**Stakeholder education:**
"Draft talking points for a presentation to [hospital social workers/law enforcement/community organizations] about how APS works and when to make a referral."

**Program performance narrative:**
"Help me write a narrative about our APS program's outcomes for [annual report/board presentation/state partner briefing]. Key data points: [insert aggregated, de-identified data]."

**Building a case for APS resources:**
"California's APS system is under-resourced relative to caseload standards. Help me make the case for additional APS staffing. Key evidence: [describe generally]. Audience: [county leadership/board]."
