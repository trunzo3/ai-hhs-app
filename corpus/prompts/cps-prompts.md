# CPS and Child Welfare Prompts for HHS Managers

## Important Data Safety Note

Child welfare work involves highly sensitive client information. Before using any AI tool for CPS-related tasks, run a Red/Yellow/Green assessment. Client names, case numbers, dates of birth, identifying addresses, and specific case details are Red — do not include them in AI prompts. Many tasks can be accomplished with hypothetical or generalized descriptions that don't identify specific clients or families.

## Policy and Regulatory Tasks (Green)

**Summarize an ACL or policy update:**
"Summarize this CDSS All-County Letter in plain language for CPS supervisors. Include: what's changing, what we're required to do differently, key deadlines, and any ambiguous requirements that need legal or policy clarification."

**Explain a regulation to staff:**
"Explain [specific regulation or requirement] in plain language for frontline CPS workers. Use examples that would be familiar in a CPS context. Avoid jargon."

**Prepare for a state audit or review:**
"Help me prepare for a [CDSS/federal] review of our [specific program area]. What documents and data should we gather? What questions are reviewers likely to ask? What are common findings in this area?"

## Supervisory and Management Tasks (Green-Yellow)

**Staff training topics:**
"Generate 5 training scenarios for new CPS workers focused on [specific area — initial assessment, safety planning, family engagement, documentation]. The scenarios should reflect common challenges without including real case details."

**Performance feedback framework:**
"Help me draft a framework for giving CPS supervisors feedback on their coaching of workers. What dimensions matter most? What behaviors indicate strong supervisory practice?"

**Meeting agenda for a complex situation:**
"Create an agenda for a [CFT/TDM/supervisory consultation] focused on a general situation where [describe scenario type without client details]. What topics should we cover to be thorough?"

## Leadership and Communication Tasks (Green)

**Community or stakeholder communication:**
"Draft a statement for [community partners/families/the board] about our child welfare program's approach to [specific topic — family engagement, safety, culturally responsive practice]."

**Data narrative:**
"Help me tell the story behind this data [provide aggregated, de-identified data]. What do the numbers suggest? How should I frame this for leadership?"

**Building a case for resources:**
"Help me build a case for additional [staffing/training/technology] in our CPS program. The key points are [describe generally]. The audience is [county leadership/board/state partners]."
