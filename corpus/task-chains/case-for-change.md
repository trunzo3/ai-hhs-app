# Task Chain: Build a Case for Change

## Task Type
Synthesize

## Purpose
HHS managers often need to persuade others to support a new approach, program, policy, or resource request. Building a compelling case requires combining data, narrative, and strategic framing. This task chain helps managers synthesize the evidence they have and present it in a way that moves decision makers.

## Intake Question
"What change are you trying to make, and who needs to be convinced? Give me the basics and I'll help you build the case."

## Full Prompt Chain

**Role:** Act as a policy advocate and strategic communications expert who has worked extensively in public sector human services.

**Instruction:** Help me build a persuasive case for [change] for [audience]. The case should:
1. Open with the problem and why it matters (what's at stake)
2. Establish the evidence base (data, best practices, precedent)
3. Present the proposed change and why it addresses the problem
4. Address the most likely objections
5. Make a clear ask

**Context:** [User describes: what change they're proposing, who decides, what the audience cares about, what data or evidence they have, what objections they expect]

**Constraints:** Make the argument in [leadership/board/county executive/state partner] terms. Focus on impact and outcomes, not process. Be direct. Don't bury the ask.

**Output format:** Structured argument document, then a one-paragraph executive summary version.

## Power Follow-Up Options
- "What's the strongest case against this?"
- "Make the opening more urgent"
- "Simplify this for a five-minute presentation"
- "What evidence am I missing?"
- "Turn this into talking points for a meeting"

## Common Case Types in HHS

**Budget requests:** Frame around return on investment, cost of inaction, and evidence from comparable counties. Be specific about what the money buys.

**Program changes:** Frame around outcomes for clients, efficiency gains, and alignment with state priorities. Show what success looks like and how you'll measure it.

**Staffing changes:** Frame around caseload standards, staff safety, and the cost of turnover vs. the cost of appropriate staffing.

**Policy changes:** Frame around legal compliance, client outcomes, and operational feasibility. Be honest about implementation challenges and how you'll address them.
