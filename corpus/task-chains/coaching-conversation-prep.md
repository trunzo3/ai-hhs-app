# Task Chain: Preparing for a Difficult Conversation

## Task Type
Prepare

## Purpose
HHS managers face difficult conversations constantly: performance issues, team conflict, delivering bad news, pushing back on leadership, navigating community relationships. The quality of these conversations depends heavily on preparation. This task chain helps managers think through their approach before the conversation happens.

## Intake Question
"Who's the conversation with, and what's the situation? I'll help you plan your approach."

## Full Prompt Chain

**Role:** Act as an executive coach with expertise in difficult workplace conversations, particularly in public sector human services contexts.

**Instruction:** Help me prepare for a difficult conversation using the following approach:
1. Clarify my goal: what outcome am I trying to achieve?
2. Identify the other person's likely perspective and concerns
3. Anticipate how they might respond (including resistance or defensiveness)
4. Suggest an opening approach
5. Identify 2-3 things I should avoid saying or doing
6. Offer a suggested structure for the conversation

**Context:** [User describes: who the conversation is with, what the situation is, what they've tried before, what outcome they want]

**Constraints:** Focus on practical preparation, not theory. Acknowledge the complexity of the relationship. Don't assume I know how to do this — be specific and direct.

**Output format:** Structured preparation guide with Opening, Key Messages, Anticipate/Respond sections, and Things to Avoid.

## Data Safety Note
This is typically a Yellow task. It's helpful to describe the situation in general terms (e.g., "an employee who has been missing deadlines") rather than including the employee's name or identifying details. The AI doesn't need to know who the person is to help you prepare for the conversation.

## Power Follow-Up Options
- "Help me practice by role-playing as the other person"
- "What am I not thinking about?"
- "Give me an opening sentence I could actually say"
- "What's the strongest case against my approach?"
- "Simplify this — what are the two or three things I actually need to remember?"

## Common Difficult Conversation Types in HHS

**Performance issues:** Focus on specific behaviors and their impact, not personality. Be clear about expectations going forward. Document the conversation.

**Delivering bad news (to staff, clients, community):** Lead with the fact. Explain why. Acknowledge the impact. Offer what support is available. Be honest about uncertainty.

**Pushing back on leadership:** Focus on impact and risk, not personal disagreement. Come with a counter-proposal, not just a rejection. Make the stakes clear.

**Team conflict:** Separate the behavior from the person. Focus on shared goals. Avoid triangulating through others.
